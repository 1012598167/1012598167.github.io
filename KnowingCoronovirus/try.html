What is COVID-19?
Since the middle of December 2019, there have been unexplained pneumonia cases with fever, fatigue, cough and poor breath as the main symptoms in Wuhan, China. The governments at all levels and the administrative departments of health and health attach great importance to them. They have organized disease control institutions, medical institutions and scientific research institutes to carry out investigation, treatment and collaborative research, and quickly determined that the pathogen of such cases is a new type of coronary The novel coronavirus pneumonia is identified as 2019-nCoV by the WHO (WHO).Now it is called COVID-19.
Coronavirus is a single strand RNA virus with no segments, belonging to the positive coronavirus subfamily of the coronaviridae of the order nidoviridae. According to the characteristics of serotypes and genomes, the coronavirus subfamily is divided into four genera: A, β, γ and δ. Coronaviruses belong to the family coronaviridae.
Coronavirus is a kind of virus. It is named for its protruding process on the envelope, which looks like a corolla. Most of the coronaviruses infect animals. At present, there are three types of coronaviruses isolated from human: common coronavirus 229E, OC43 and SARS CoV. There are 6 kinds of coronaviruses known to infect human: 229E and nl63 in α genus, OC43 and hku1 in β genus, mers cov in Middle East respiratory syndrome and SARS CoV in severe acute respiratory syndrome. A novel coronavirus isolated from lower respiratory tract of patients with unknown pneumonia in Wuhan recently was named as 2019-nCoV, a new coronavirus. WHO is a new type of coronavirus. The novel coronavirus novel coronavirus (2019-nCoV) novel coronavirus (SARS-CoV) is now known (January 2020). The 6 coronaviruses are similar to the new coronavirus (2019-nCoV) genome sequence.
The homology analysis of the gene sequence shows that the new coronavirus is 79.5% similar to that of the coronavirus. The new coronavirus can be classified into the beta coronavirus at present (January 2020).
Where do it come from
Many wild animals may carry pathogens and become the vector of some infectious diseases. Civet cat, bat, bamboo rat and badger are the common hosts of coronavirus. YThere are many similarities between the outbreak of viral pneumonia in Wuhan and the outbreak of SARS in Guangdong Province in 2003. They all occur in winter.
The initial occurrence is caused by the contact between human and live animals in the animal market, and all of them are caused by unknown coronavirus.Because the novel coronavirus's novel coronavirus's evolutionary neighbors and outgroup have been found in various bats, it is speculated that the natural host of Wuhan's new coronavirus may also be bats. Novel coronavirus, like the SARS coronavirus that led to 2003, is likely to have unknown intermediate host vectors in the process of propagation from bat to human.

Who are susceptible to COVID-19
Whether or not it is infected mainly depends on the chance of contact.
It is not that the risk of infection will be lower in people with strong resistance. Children have less contact opportunities, and the probability of infection is low; the same contact opportunities, the elderly, people with chronic diseases and people with poor resistance are more likely to be infected.
People are generally susceptible. Novel coronavirus pneumonia can occur in people with low immune function and normal immune function, which is related to the amount of virus exposure. If a large number of viruses are exposed at one time, even if the immune function is normal, the disease may also occur. For people with poor immune function, such as the elderly, pregnant women or people with liver and kidney dysfunction, the disease progress is relatively faster and the severity is higher.

SYMPTOM
Novel coronavirus pneumonia is mainly manifested by fever. Some early patients can not have fever, only chills and respiratory symptoms. They can combine mild dry cough, fatigue, poor breathing, diarrhea, and other symptoms such as runny nose and expectoration. Half of the patients developed dyspnea one week later, and the patients with severe dyspnea progressed rapidly. Within a few days, acute respiratory distress syndrome, septic shock, metabolic acidosis and coagulation dysfunction were found. Some patients have mild onset symptoms and no fever. Most patients have a good prognosis, and a few patients are in critical condition or even die

If COVID-19 attacked you
It is necessary to go to the local designated medical institutions for investigation and diagnosis and treatment in a timely manner. If a novel coronavirus is suspected of infection, the disease should be described in detail when going to hospital. In particular, doctors should be informed of recent travel and residence history in Wuhan, the history of contact between pneumonia patients or suspected patients, and animal contact history. It shouldbe noted that surgical masks should be worn throughout the treatment process to protect themselves and others.
